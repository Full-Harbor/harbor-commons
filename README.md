# Harbor Commons

**Live site:** https://commons.fullharbor.org

Harbor Commons is an open data platform for sailing and maritime nonprofit infrastructure.
It makes public records legible for practitioners, boards, funders, and researchers who need a field-level view but do not have in-house data capacity.

## Why it exists

Small organizations are routinely asked to make strategic decisions with almost no shared infrastructure.
Harbor Commons turns that problem inside out by organizing public nonprofit records into a field-usable reference layer.

Current scope:
- **4.2M IRS filings** in the underlying public-record universe
- **2,000+ sailing and maritime organizations** surfaced for field analysis
- **13 filing years** of longitudinal nonprofit infrastructure context

Sailing is Example 1. The method is portable.

## Who can use this

- **Practitioners** who need peer benchmarks, organization lookup, or funding context
- **Boards and committees** who need a broader view of the field
- **Funders and researchers** who want receipts-first, source-linked public infrastructure
- **Other sectors** looking for a worked example of field-level nonprofit intelligence

## What this repo is

This public repo is the product-facing documentation layer for Harbor Commons.
It explains what the platform does, how the data is approached, and how the public artifact fits inside the wider Full Harbor capacity-commons thesis.

## Supporting artifacts

- [`irs-990-extract`](https://github.com/Full-Harbor/irs-990-extract) — reusable XML section extractors for IRS 990 filings
- [`pdf-goat`](https://github.com/Full-Harbor/pdf-goat) — canonical PDF ingestion pipeline for OCR, chunking, embeddings, and provenance
- [`full-harbor`](https://github.com/Full-Harbor/full-harbor) — the umbrella narrative for the broader shared-infrastructure project

## Public discipline

Harbor Commons is built on public records and public-facing methods.
Private scoring, hiring research, grantee flags, and application-specific judgment stay out of the public layer.
